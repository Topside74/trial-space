Al Hidaya Islamic Academy

Al-Hidaya Islamic Academy, Kalamassery, was established in 2015 as a unified and unanimous decision of the Njalakam Muslim Jama'ath. The institution initially began as a traditional masjid dars (mosque-based religious education center), following the classical Darsi methodology. Scholars from both southern and northern Kerala, known for their wisdom and vision, played a pivotal role in the growth and elevation of this Jama'ath. The leadership came from prominent Islamic families and socially respected elders of the community. The Masjid Dars officially began in 1923 . It was later transformed into an Islamic Academy in2015. it attained the prestigious affiliation with Darul Huda Islamic University, becoming one of its recognized institutions. Today, AHIA stands as a proud symbol of South Kerala’s Islamic academic excellence and shines as a golden feather in the cap of Kochi, the smart city. Njalakam Muslim Juma Masjid

Njalakam Muslim Juma Masjid, situated in Kalamassery and built around the year1920 , is in proximity to metro city Kochi and CUSAT (Cochin University of Science and Technology). This mosque acts as a meeting point for community members, students from different parts of the country, and tourists. It becomes colorful during Jumu’ah prayers, Eidfest celebrations, and Iftar in Ramadhan. With the passage of time, the mosque has gone through cycles of renovations and development activities. The Mahal, comprising more than 1500 families, has an active role in the socio-cultural and philanthropic life of the central Kerala region. In addition to initiatives like providing housing for the homeless, patient care, elder support, widow pensions, interest-free loan schemes, marriage assistance programs for underprivileged women, and ambulance services, the Najath Public School, Njalakam Convention Center, and Al-Hidaya Islamic Academy are among the proud institutions run by this Mahall (local Muslim community). The current mosque complex is being developed to include five floors, with facilities such as parking, a library, an archaeology museum, a dedicated block for women travelers, a funeral care unit, a clinic and medical store, a spacious area for breaking fast during Ramadan, a conference hall, and an Islamic bookstore.

Mission

To develop an Islamic educational framework that nurtures individuals who are conscious of their ultimate responsibilities—towards Allah, themselves, their families, and society—by practicing Islam and promoting religious scholarship. This mission is aligned with the pursuit of knowledge about various subjects and realities of life, guided by the sacred Qur’an, the legacy of the Prophet (ﷺ), and the Islamic intellectual heritage. The aim is to unify the pursuit of such knowledge with a deep-rooted Islamic understanding of truth and purpose.

Vision

The vision of Darul Huda is to become a center of excellence in Islamic education that seeks to revive the spirit of scholarly mastery in Islamic sciences. It aspires to establish research hubs in various Islamic disciplines, preserve the intellectual heritage, and ultimately shape a generation of scholars (Ulama) who are the true spiritual heirs of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ).
